CCTV

Wellington City Council (WCC) operates a network of closed circuit television (CCTV) surveillance cameras in Wellington, known as Camerabase. The surveillance system is monitered by trained staff.

Camerabase is a Council-led initiative and is strongly suppoerted through WCC's partnership with WEllington Cdentral Police.

The use of CCTV cameras aims to make Wellington a safe city though activities that target and prevent crime and public disorder, ensure efficient responses from emergency services, and increase public safety.

The collection and monitoring of footage and data is carried out in accordance with the Privacy Act 1993 and WEllington City CCTV Guidelines.

All cameras are marked with a sign that says "Crime prevention and safety cameras are operating in this area".

Cables

Fiber-optica cables are similar to electric cables in assemblu, but instead carry one or more optical fibres made from glass that are used to carry light

These cables support much of the world's internet, cable television, and phone systems and is being rolled out around New Zealand at an increasing rate.

There are a number of ways that fiber-optic cables can be installed. These methods include an areal cable, an underground pipe, mounting on a fence or retaining wall, or burying the cable in a grass verge/garden or underneath a driveway or path.

Power lines

Wellington Electricity uses a 33 kV sub-transmission network, with 11 kV high-voltage distribution and 230/400 V low-voltage distribution.

The network comprises a high percentage of underground cabling, with 66% of the sub-transmission circuits being cabled.

THe majority of electricity used in Wellington from the national grid and Transpower substations, as well as two landfill gas stations, a gas fired cogeneration facility and a single wind turbine.

Antennas and Satellites

An antenna is a device used to transmit and/or recieve electromagnetic waves.

An antenna must be tunes to the same frequency band as the radio system to which it is connected, otherwise rections and/or transmission will be impaired

The two types of antenna generally used in New Zealand are UHF aerials and satellite dishes.

One of the most popular uses for these technologies is free to air or subscription television services.

Manholes

A manhole is an opening to a confined space, such as a shaft, utility vault, or large vessel.

Manholes are often used as an access point for an underground public utility, allowing inspection, maintenance, and system upgrades.

These services include access to underground cable networks.
